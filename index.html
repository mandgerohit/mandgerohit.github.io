About

Jianzhuo Wu (who goes by the alias "Eric" or "J. Wu") is an Atlanta-based web developer and designer who likes to find ways to integrate aesthetic designs and streamlined code into innovative solutions. He can often be found glued to screen sipping coffee and discerning through lines of code in the browser console, clacking away in Sublime Text, or fine-tuning a design in Photoshop.

Since receiving his Bachelor of Science at Georgia Tech, J. Wu has been employed at a local Atlanta company where he uses his expertise in design and development to build a number of web applications. In his free time, J. Wu likes to work on personal side projects and open source projects on Github.

Workflow and Process

I take great care every step of the way ensure no funny business after deployment, no variables left unchecked, and no pixels out of place.

To start off, I consult much search engines and knowledge bases to gain an understanding of the materials.

The brainstorming stage is the most active and whimsical stage of the development process. I grab my notepad and begin sketching schematics

and wireframes of the design, often scribbling notes here and there on interactive components and animations.

Jumping straight into design and development, I would glue myself to my computer for hours fine-tuning design and assets, and clacking out code.

Once all the i's are dotted, t's crossed, and final tuneup and systems check completed, the finished product is deployed to sour into Cyberspace!
